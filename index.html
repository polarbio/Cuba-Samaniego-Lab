Engineering genetic circuits
that behave as neural networks

Welcome to the Cuba-Samaniego Lab at Carnegie Mellon University. We design and study synthetic genetic circuits that combine principles of machine learning and feedback control to create new computational capabilities in living systems.

Learn More

What does the lab do?

As an interdisciplinary research laboratory, our goal is to make the paradigms of Machine Learning and Feedback Control accessible and readily applicable in Biology. We focus on developing computational, theoretical, and experimental tools to facilitate this integration across various biological systems, ranging from single cells to multicellular levels.

Outreach

The Cuba-Samaniego Lab is committed to advancing education and collaboration in the fields of synthetic biology, systems biology, and biomolecular control. We organize international computational workshops and mentoring initiatives to make quantitative modeling and control theory accessible to the biological sciences.

Engineering Biological Control Programs (EBCP) Workshop

The Engineering Biological Control Programs Workshop (EBCP) is an international virtual course focused on the computational design and analysis of synthetic genetic circuits. Participants learn how to model, simulate, and analyze biological control systems using modern computational tools, with an emphasis on applying feedback and control principles to genetic regulation.

The workshop combines interactive lectures, guided coding exercises, and open discussions to help students bridge concepts between biology and systems engineering.
